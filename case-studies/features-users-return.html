I significantly boosted user retention and saved teachers years of planning time. Here's how.

The Problem

Teachers were overwhelmed by an expanding content library.

On the Inquisitive platform, the content library was growing quickly. Teachers struggled to find, save and organise what they needed for planning.

  • "How can I save units I'm searching?"
  • "I keep getting lost trying to bookmark content."

They needed a way to manage planning without having to navigate the whole site every time.

The Goal

Teachers needed clear, easy-to-access planning tools aligned with the curriculum.

The primary audience was teachers in Australia and the US. Their needs were:

  • Simple, clear planning aligned with curriculum standards.
  • Quick access to teaching content.

Manual workarounds were time-consuming and didn't scale.

How I did it

Leading end-to-end research and design, collaborating closely with product and development teams.

I led the design and research for this work. Activities included:

  • Analyzing jargon queries.
  • Conducting interviews and surveys with teachers.
  • Prototyping and usability testing.

I worked with the Head of Product and development, using Figma, UserTesting, Dovetail and Zoom. Constraints included desktop, tablet and mobile, and the shift to online teaching during COVID.

The Process

Incremental improvements guided by continuous feedback transformed the tool from simple bookmarking to powerful planning.

Starting Simple

We began with a straightforward bookmarking-style feature so teachers could save content without extra complexity.

Team-Based Organisation

The content structure evolved to support how teams actually plan and share units.

Prioritizing Units

Research showed that unit-level organisation mattered more than individual resources, so we prioritised units in the experience.

Featureless Access

We introduced a unit-restricted tool and a slide-out drawer so teachers could preview and work with units without leaving their flow.

Ongoing Iteration

We relied on continuous feedback and regular interviews to refine the product over time.

The Solution

A simple, intuitive Planner aligned closely with teachers' real-world workflows.

Key Features

We delivered a set of core features that matched how teachers plan:

  • Unit-Based Organisation: Direct alignment with teacher workflows.
  • Term-Based: Structured planning across school terms.
  • Dynamic Overview: Instant unit previews, reducing navigation friction.
  • Flexible Reordering: Easy drag and drop adjustments.

Simplicity and clarity were the main design goals.

The Outcome

Dramatically improved user retention and satisfaction with clear, measurable impacts.

The Planner became a central feature: over 90% of teachers use it at least weekly.

  • Over half of our users use it regularly.
  • Users report dramatically improved planning efficiency.
  • Support tickets related to planning dropped completely.
  • Consistent feedback praises the Planner as "essential" and "a lifesaver".

This is all real feedback sent through Planner's "give us feedback" mechanism.

The End Result

Incremental, user-focused improvements can lead to exceptional outcomes.

Thousands of teachers have saved substantial planning time. The work showed how incremental design, grounded in user research and feedback, can deliver strong results and make a core product feature feel essential.
